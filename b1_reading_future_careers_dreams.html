Section 5: Reading Passage 1 — Future Careers

Read the text carefully. Then answer the questions that follow.
In the past, many people chose jobs that were familiar to their families, such as teaching, farming, nursing, or working in an office. Today, the world of work is changing quickly. New technology, environmental challenges, and global communication are creating careers that did not exist before. For example, some people now work as app designers, digital marketers, drone operators, or renewable energy engineers.

However, choosing a future career is not only about following trends. Students also need to understand their interests, skills, and values. A person who enjoys solving problems may become an engineer or a data analyst. Someone who likes helping others may choose medicine, education, or social work. Creative students may prefer careers in media, design, or writing.

Experts believe that future workers will need strong communication, teamwork, and critical thinking skills. They will also need to keep learning throughout their lives because jobs will continue to change. The best career is not always the most popular one. It is the career that allows a person to grow, contribute to society, and feel proud of their work.

Section 6: Reading Passage 2 — In Your Dreams

Read the text. Then choose the best answers.
Everyone has dreams for the future. Some students dream of becoming doctors, pilots, teachers, artists, athletes, or business owners. Others dream of travelling around the world, helping their families, or making a positive difference in their community. Dreams are important because they give people hope and direction.

However, a dream does not become real by wishing only. It needs planning, effort, and patience. For example, a student who dreams of becoming a doctor must study science carefully, improve communication skills, and learn how to care for people. A student who wants to become a professional football player needs regular training, discipline, and a healthy lifestyle.

Dreams can also change as people grow. This is normal. Sometimes, students discover new talents or interests. They may meet inspiring people or learn about careers they never imagined before. The most important thing is not to give up when the path becomes difficult. Successful people often face challenges, but they use these challenges to become stronger. A dream is like a map: it shows where you want to go, but you must take the steps yourself.
